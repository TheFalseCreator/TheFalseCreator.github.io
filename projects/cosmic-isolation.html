Speculative Cosmology / Research Framework

Cosmic
Isolation

A theoretical exploration of Cosmic Isolation as a geometric counterpart to black holes in early-universe models, with the goal of building a clearer framework for mentor review.

Status

Seeking academic collaboration

Focus

Spacetime, expansion, singularities

Need

Physics mentor feedback

Output

Framework and diagrams

The Story

Cosmic Isolation is the most speculative project in the portfolio. It grew from curiosity about black holes, singularities, and the early universe. The current goal is not to present it as finished science, but to document the idea carefully enough for expert feedback.

Components Used

  • Conceptual geometry and spacetime diagrams.
  • Comparisons with black-hole ideas and singularity questions.
  • Written framework for assumptions and definitions.
  • Visual models for expansion and isolation.

People and Context

This is the project most in need of institutional support. The next phase depends on physicists, teachers, or research mentors who can challenge the assumptions and help separate promising ideas from weak ones.

What I Learned

Big theoretical ideas need humility. The stronger move is to make the idea readable, define the claims, and invite critique rather than pretending the framework is complete.

Next Steps

Create cleaner diagrams, write a short concept paper, collect feedback from physics mentors, and refine or reject parts of the idea based on critique.
