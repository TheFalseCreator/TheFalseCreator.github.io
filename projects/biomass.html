The Story

The project began with a practical environmental problem: agricultural residue contributes to air pollution when burned, but the same biomass can be treated as a material resource. I explored biodegradable packaging as a way to turn waste into useful infrastructure while giving farmers a more productive alternative.

Components Used

  • Agricultural waste and biomass substrate.
  • Binding and forming process for packaging sheets.
  • Material testing for rigidity, use, and presentation.
  • Conductive composite exploration using pollution-derived particulates.

People and Context

This project was shaped through school-level research, presentation rounds, and feedback from competition evaluators. The wider context was Delhi's recurring pollution crisis and the search for usable student-led sustainability ideas.

What I Learned

A project becomes stronger when the science, user need, and economic story support each other. The prototype is not only a material experiment; it is also an argument for circular design.

Next Steps

Improve documentation, add real prototype images, test biodegradability and strength more formally, and seek mentors who can help validate the composite and packaging claims.
