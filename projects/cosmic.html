A proposal for Cosmic Isolation as a geometric counterpart: a region of spacetime that expands outward rather than collapsing inward, offering an alternative way to think about singularities.

A young framework looking for serious critique.

Singularities in black hole and Big Bang models are mathematically problematic. Cosmic Isolation is a speculative attempt to imagine a geometric counterpart to collapse: a spacetime region expanding outward rather than concentrating inward.

The Problem

Singularities can mark where current models break down.

The Process

Define assumptions, sketch geometry, compare against known physics, invite critique.
