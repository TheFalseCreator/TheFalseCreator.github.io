Civic Technology / Urban Systems

Raksita

A solution concept for urban waterlogging using predictive modeling, real-time sensors, and response-oriented civic design.

Status

Presented competition concept

Recognition

1st position, Niamat Rai State Competition

Focus

Waterlogging, sensors, civic response

Output

Prototype logic and presentation

The Story

Raksita grew out of a city problem that is visible every monsoon: waterlogging damages roads, slows transport, and creates unsafe conditions. The project imagines a way to move from reactive cleanup to early warning and better planning.

Components Used

  • Water-level sensing concept.
  • Predictive modeling for risk estimation.
  • Real-time alert and monitoring logic.
  • Urban drainage and response mapping.

People and Context

The project was presented for competition evaluation and shaped by Delhi's recurring infrastructure challenges. It sits between engineering, civic planning, and public service.

What I Learned

Civic-tech projects need more than a clever device. They need clear deployment logic, data pathways, maintenance thinking, and a realistic user: who receives the alert and acts on it?

Next Steps

Add diagrams, test a small sensor model, define a dashboard flow, and document how city authorities or communities could use the system.
