Civic Technology / Urban Water

RAKSITA

Predictive modelling and real-time sensor logic for urban waterlogging response.

Year

Grade 11

Domain

Civic Tech

Award

1st, Niamat Rai State Competition

Status

Competition concept

From flooded streets to early warning.

Raksita responds to Delhi's recurring waterlogging problem. The idea combines water-level sensing, prediction, and communication so communities and authorities can act before disruption becomes damage.

The Problem

Urban flooding arrives quickly and responses are often reactive.

The Process

Map risk, sense water levels, predict severity, and trigger alerts.

The People I Met

Competition evaluators, teachers, and civic-minded peers helped refine the framing.

The Outcome

1st position at the Niamat Rai State Competition.

Components / Materials Used

System parts.

Water-Level Sensors

Detect rising water levels in vulnerable areas.

Prediction Model

Uses environmental and location data to estimate risk.

Alert System

Communicates warnings to decision-makers and communities.

Key Outcomes

Why it matters.

  1. Turns a civic problem into a measurable system.
  2. Connects prediction with real-time response.
  3. Frames student innovation as public infrastructure thinking.
