Health Technology / Wellbeing

Neuro
Health

A mental-health monitoring system concept integrating EXG sensors to identify high-stress patterns and support timely intervention.

Status

Design summit concept

Recognition

INR 20K, Humanistic Design Summit 2

Focus

Stress, signals, support

Ethic

Careful, human-centered monitoring

The Story

NeuroHealth began with a human problem: stress is often noticed too late. The project explores whether physiological signals can help identify high-stress states earlier, while still treating mental health with care, privacy, and responsibility.

Components Used

  • EXG sensor concept for physiological signals.
  • Stress-pattern detection logic.
  • User support and alert pathway.
  • Humanistic design framing for mental-health use.

People and Context

The project was developed for Humanistic Design Summit 2, where the key challenge was not only technical feasibility, but identifying a meaningful problem and proposing a responsible path toward support.

What I Learned

Health technology needs empathy before engineering. A good system must avoid overclaiming, protect users, and make intervention feel supportive rather than invasive.

Next Steps

Clarify sensor assumptions, add a user journey, document privacy safeguards, and build a more polished interface concept.
