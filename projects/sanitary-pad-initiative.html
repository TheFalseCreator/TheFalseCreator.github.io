Community Impact / Hygiene Access

Sanitary Pad
Machine Initiative

A student-led service project focused on funding and installing a sanitary pad machine through Rotary Club work, with the goal of improving hygiene access.

Status

Implemented initiative

Recognition

Rotary Club national recognition

Focus

Hygiene, access, dignity

Role

Funding and student leadership

The Story

This project was about turning leadership into practical service. The aim was to support menstrual hygiene access by helping secure funding and move an idea from intent to installation.

Components Used

  • Sanitary pad machine installation planning.
  • Fundraising and stakeholder coordination.
  • Rotary Club support and domain leadership.
  • Implementation thinking around access and maintenance.

People and Context

The project was connected to Rotary Club work and broader community service. It required communication, trust, fundraising, and the ability to bring people together around a practical welfare goal.

What I Learned

Community projects are judged by whether they actually help people. Leadership matters most when it becomes logistics, follow-through, and shared responsibility.
